# Exploratory Analysis of Oxidative Stress–Associated Gene Expression Across Adult *C. elegans* Neurons

## Overview

This project explores the expression of oxidative stress–associated genes across adult neuronal populations in *Caenorhabditis elegans* using publicly available single-cell RNA sequencing data from the CeNGEN consortium.

Oxidative stress is a major contributor to cellular aging, neuronal dysfunction, and neurodegeneration. Because neuronal populations differ in function, metabolism, and vulnerability to stress, understanding cell-type-specific expression of oxidative stress pathways may provide insight into mechanisms of neuronal resilience and susceptibility.

Using transcriptomic data from adult *C. elegans* neurons, this analysis investigates how genes involved in oxidative stress regulation and insulin signaling are distributed across neuronal cell types, with particular attention to dopaminergic neuron subclasses.

---

## Research Question

How do oxidative stress–associated genes vary across adult neuronal populations, and do dopaminergic neuron subclasses exhibit distinct stress-response expression patterns?

---

## Biological Background

Oxidative stress occurs when the production of reactive oxygen species exceeds a cell's ability to neutralize them. Persistent oxidative damage has been implicated in aging and numerous neurodegenerative diseases.

To investigate neuronal stress-response pathways, three genes were selected for analysis:

### age-1

Encodes a phosphatidylinositol 3-kinase involved in insulin/IGF-1 signaling, a conserved pathway regulating aging, metabolism, and stress resistance.

### sod-3

Encodes a mitochondrial superoxide dismutase that protects cells from oxidative damage by detoxifying reactive oxygen species.

### skn-1

Encodes a transcription factor that regulates antioxidant and detoxification pathways and serves as a major regulator of oxidative stress responses.

---

## Dataset

**Source:** CeNGEN Adult Single-Cell RNA Sequencing Dataset

**Organism:** *Caenorhabditis elegans*

This analysis examined gene expression across numerous adult neuronal populations, including sensory neurons, interneurons, motor neurons, and dopaminergic neuron subclasses (ADE, CEP, and PDE).

---

## Methods

1. Retrieved single-cell RNA sequencing expression data from the CeNGEN consortium.
2. Selected oxidative stress–associated genes (*age-1*, *sod-3*, and *skn-1*).
3. Examined transcript abundance across adult neuronal cell types.
4. Visualized expression patterns using a single-cell RNA-seq dot plot.
5. Performed focused analysis of dopaminergic neuron subclasses to investigate potential differences in oxidative stress-related gene expression.

---

## Key Findings

* Oxidative stress–associated genes demonstrated distinct expression patterns across neuronal populations.
* Expression varied considerably among cell types, supporting transcriptomic heterogeneity within the nervous system.
* Dopaminergic neuron subclasses exhibited different expression profiles despite sharing a common neurotransmitter identity.
* ADE neurons demonstrated the strongest expression of both *age-1* and *sod-3*, while PDE neurons showed little to no detectable expression.
* These findings suggest that oxidative stress-response pathways may be differentially utilized among neuronal populations.
